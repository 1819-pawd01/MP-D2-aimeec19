HTML Cheat Sheet
Element Description Example
Comments When you want to comment on the html, but don't want it to show up on the web page. <!--comment-- >
Title The title of the page that shows up on the tab. <title> </title>
Paragraphs How to define the paragraphs <p> </p>
Headings How to define the headings and show document structure. Can be in 6 different sizes. <h1> </h1>
<h2> </h2>....
Bolding Bolding certain words or sentences. <b> </b>
or
<strong> </strong>
Italics To italizes words or sentences. <i></i>
Emphasis To bold words and emphasize them. <em></em>
Blank Line To make a blank line and help space things out. <br />
Horizontal Line To make a noticeable horizontal line. <hr />
Blockquote To make a blockquote inside a paragraph. <blockquote></blockquote>
A Short Quote To make a short quote. <q></q>
Strikethrough To strikethrough a word on the page. <del></del>
Underline To underline a word or sentence. <u></u>
Copyright Symbol © To copyright your page in the footer. &copy;
& Symbol To create the & symbol and make it show up on the web page. &amp;

CSS Cheat Sheet
Element Description Example
Font Size To change the font size. subject {font-size: ;}
Text Color Change the color of the text. subject {color: ;}
Text Decorations To decorate the text like underline, bolding, or overline. subject {text-decoration: ;}
Letter Spacing To space out the characters in the text. subject {letter-spacing: ;}
Text Shadow Adds shadow to text. subject {text-shadow: ;}
Font Style Mostly used to specify italics. subject {font-style: ;}
Font Weight Sets how thick or thin characters in text are displayed. subject {font-weight: ;}
Font Varient Defines whether or not text is displayed in small caps or not. subject {font-varient: ;}
Background Color To set up the background color of your webpage. subject {background-color: ;}
Text Alignment To align the text where every you want to start it. subject {text-align: ;}
